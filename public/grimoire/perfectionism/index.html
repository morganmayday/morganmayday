notes on perfectionism

A. - 1 January 2026

this is notes. loose stuff. don’t try to find a beam in here, this is stream of thought.

I talk about the gods, I am an atheist. But I am an artist too, and therefore a liar. Distrust everything I say. I am telling the truth. The only truth I can understand or express is, logically defined, a lie. Psychologically defined, a symbol. Aesthetically defined, a metaphor.

Ursula K. Le Guin, The Left Hand Of Darkness

hey. can i tell you a secret?

i sprawl back on the couch, sigh.

I’ve hated all of my writing, lately.

sure, there’s some that i don’t hate hate, a few monologues or prompters or vignettes for various things that I feel like are fine, y’know? it’s just…

I always feel like I can do more. do better. I see the shit Caroline writes, and I’m always blown away. she wrote Do As You Will. she’s–clever, and incisive, and she never really lets the emotions boil over onto the page unless it fits. she’s a talented fucking writer.

I can evoke a feeling. I can do that real well. It’s my strength, in writing; I can communicate what I’m feeling really really effectively. but it never feels…I am an artist and therefore a liar. I can tell you the truth of what I feel, but I can’t conjure exactly what I experience or the whole of the fact into your head. and maybe that’s a beautiful thing, that nobody ever can reach, but it always feels like failure.

and, sure, logically–logically i’m decently good at this. logically evoking that feeling is a powerful thing to do. it’s useful, it’s a component of good writing, et cetera et cetera et cetera. but i look at the writing around me and go wow.

maybe i just kinda suck.

this isn’t to say, “oh, feel bad for me.” this is to say this is–the back edge of perfectionism. yeah, it drives me forwards to make something better, at all times. yeah, it’s what’s gotten me, continually, to become more. but at the back edge, i’m not and can’t be perfect. never will be. and i resent that. like sure i don’t expect perfection of others, but it’s–me. A. Morgan. I know, in my heart of hearts, that I can do anything if I choose to. i can’t ask that of others, but i can ask it of myself.

i just usually miss the step where skills require practice.

i laugh, a little. i really, really don’t like being bad at things. that’s the perfectionism, baby! if i wanna get good at stuff, i have to be bad at it first. i have to keep reminding myself this–it ain’t like i got good at anything overnight. every skill i’ve had, i’ve learned the long way round. i forget that often, cause like. i have 20k+ hours, on average, in a lot of the shit i’m really good at. there’s shit i have 50k+ in. and that’s part of why the first learning always feels painful.

i’ve been learning to sing.
