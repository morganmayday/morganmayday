From Here to Tomorrow

I want something more than an apology to say, when I look the world in the eye. - 13 March 2026

I can’t see the line.

because there is, right? there is some line, some sequence of events and actions, that I can take, that I can advocate, to make a Better World. I believe that; I have to believe that.

but I can’t see it, can’t hold it all in my mind at once, picture what needs to be done. and that scares me. I lie awake, some nights, staring at the wall, asking–what should I do? What can I do?

and more and more I look at the world and I see horror, and I feel the razor-wire noose tighten. transgender women in prison are raped so often it’s a near-certainty. they want to outlaw adult trans care. they’ve just legalized state-level bans as i write this. you can see their line, here, right? forever war on foreign soil and the home front, the elimination of minorities except as at best slave labor, the panopticon watching with a little jesus sticker on the server rack.

and i still have to wake up every day and make breakfast.

i talked in Strategy for the Dying Future fairly optimistically. i talked about there being no future where the Abyss wins. i believed it. i kind of still do. but i see how many people are sitting on their fucking hands, i see how many people are just kind of slogging through their day to day never talking to their neighbors, and i know that I may not be able to see the line, but I also know this frozen apathy isn’t it.
