"Sorry" is all well and good, but what are you going to do about it?

I have a very strict heuristic set for apologies.\
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;--- I only apologise for things I can do better on in future, or if they're things I won't have the chance to.\
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;--- I only apologise for things I *want* to do better on in future, or if I regret them.\
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;--- I must actually follow through on my apologies.\
To violate any of these is to lie, and to paraphrase [Margaret Killjoy](https://bsky.app/profile/margaret.bsky.social), it's only ethical to lie to people it's ethical to punch.

While these are heuristics for myself, I still do expect them of others. I expect those around me to follow through on their apologies, and I expect those around me not to apologise if it's not something they're going to change. 

A large part of this outwards-facing expectation is that I actually expect those around me to take up space. To ask someone to flatten themself for me is...anathema, to what I am and want to be. I've felt the psychic burden, when you think of every bit of space you take as something to apologise for, and felt what it's like to no longer be under it. I don't want those around me to apologise for taking up space, for having lives, for having to do other things, for acting in their own self-interest---and if someone expects that of *me,* they are not someone I want in my life.

The other part of this expectation is that I also expect others to be willing to learn from their mistakes. This is a significantly harder expectation---if someone has been made to feel like they aren't allowed to take up space, that's something I'm never going to fault them, but if you are taking your self as the best possible You you can be and mistakes as either flukes or immutable flaws, you're wrong, plain and simple. There is always room to improve.
