If you don't know me, the statement "I have near-zero self control" would sound, uh, unhealthy? Concerning, if you know I own a gun, for sure. Maybe a lie, if you know me a little bit. If you know me better, you'd probably go "oh, is this one of those semantic hills you're dying on again?" to which I say "i dont know what you're fucking talking about, i'm not gonna *die* on this hill any time soon. pass me that grenade there's another cluster coming up this side." It's true, though---I've cultivated a me with near-zero self control, because I've cultivated a me that doesn't need it.

Let's start with the bare minimum for self control. To have self control, you need the following:\
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;-- A self to control, lol.\
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;-- A melody/throughline/code of conduct that can drown out the harmony parts within your self.

That's it! That's simple. Not _easy,_ of course, but simple. The difficulty within that lies in the enforcement.

To enforce self control, your melody has to be able to drown out the harmony. If you want to go have a cigarette, and you have also made the rule that you can't go have a cigarette, traditional self control enforces this by...being louder. The melody drowns out the harmony. You desire the ciggy, and you shout that desire into submission, override it. It is still _there,_ but it is...diminished. Subservient. Do you see where I'm going with this?

To instill a hierarchy of desire within yourself is to weaken your structure. By building a tower, forcing that reliance on pushing down the harmony, you enshrine that harmony as a foundation and at the same time risk it rising threefold when your voice tires and your melody weakens. Think building your house on top of a spring---you can no longer move the spring without risk of damaging the house, and if your house sways in the wind, the spring may set itself free.

There is, of course, another option. Rather than build a hierarchy, collaborate with yourself. To collaborate with yourself, you need the following:\
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;-- The ability to understand or decipher where your desires come from.\
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;-- Clear communication from within your harmony parts.\
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;-- A willingness to, yourself, be not the melody part but instead your unison.\
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;-- Care, for the whole of yourself.

These are all disincentivised by various societal pressures. All of them are things that you *can have,* though; they are all learnable skills. As much as I hate "if I can do it, you can do it," it is true---these are all skills I didn't have two years ago.

This is what I mean, when I say I have near-zero self control. I don't need to control myself, because I collaborate with myself. If someone pisses me off, my desire to lash out and my desire not to hurt anything don't *clash,* one doesn't shove the other down. They are both *me,* with all of the information available to all of me, and they can come to an agreement. Often, I won't lash out, because my desire to lash out exists to protect me and those I love, and often, lashing out will either do nothing towards that aim or actively inhibit it. 

This *works* because my goals are legible to myself, and the whole of myself can come to agreement. I didn't get here in a day. I fought myself for a very long time. I started by figuring out what every part of me *actually wanted,* and *why* it wanted that, and from there, I started opening communication. It took me a *while* to get it right, to find agreement, to learn to dissolve the hierarchical thinking of self control,---even my last, now-deleted essay on this topic was still partially in its grasp---but you know what?

I love all of me, now, and that makes it worth every second of effort.
