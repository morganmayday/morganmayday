So much antismoking propaganda focuses around one simple idea: "you'll quit if you learn it's bad for you."

This is...well, in the modern day, who cares, right? We're all gonna die sometime, might as well have smoko on the way out. Everyone I've talked to about smoking knows it'll kill us if we don't quit. The propaganda just feels like it's *babying* us. (of course, I don't think the demographic they're targeting is the working poor, just highschoolers, but it's still insulting.)

I started smoking because it would get me longer and more breaks at my last job. Full stop. Nobody was ever going to say "no, you should smoke inside in front of the customers." I kept smoking because it was good for calming me when I felt like I was going to fucking explode from the stress. I didn't do it *often,* because I like having lungs, I wasn't under enough stress to feel it was warranted, and cigarettes frankly kinda fucking suck. I still don't smoke much, if at all, but I do crave it sometimes.

This essay isn't, quite, about cigarettes. 

I like who I am drunk...*fine?* I talk more, which is helpful sometimes; I tend to not mind the harshness of hectic or unpleasant sensory environments as much, which is helpful sometimes; I feel more willing to be vulnerable, which is helpful sometimes. Drinking has never caused me problems, except that one time I forgot my parents were in the room and quoted that one tumblr post about "givin her the ol' JFK, messy head in the backseat of her car" to my sibling, and even that was more funny than a problem. I like the taste of alcohol, too, or at least the way it changes the taste of other drinks---I don't like colas near as much now that I've had a good rum and coke, they're too *sweet.* I don't drink enough to get hungover or black out, I don't drink more than maybe three a week at the absolute outside, etc. You get the picture. It's not a problem for me.

I'm going mostly sober anyways. I'm not going to, like, full-on quit; with friends as a social occasion, on rare occasions as a tool in my work, I don't plan on stopping, but that's going to be bringing the number of drinks I have a year from maybe 100 to maybe a dozen. It's not sobriety, but it's a mindset shift, for sure. 

This is an individual choice, and I thought about it a while before making it, so I want to share my rationale.

One: anything I can do with drugs like alcohol or tobacco, I can do just fine without it. If I want to lower my inhibitions, I can just do that myself. If I want to socialise with my friends, I don't need to be intoxicated for them to like me. There are edge cases in which substances are actually useful tools, but restricting the tool to the little annoying-to-open compartment of your toolbox means you aren't going to reach for it first.

Two: I like being [sharp](/grimoire/sharp-edges). Substances...don't facilitate that. I can't think in a straight, clear line as well drunk---if at all---and yeah, that's the point sometimes, but honestly? I'd do better just having a sharper edge with which to deal with situations I don't want to be thinking in. The lure of the anaesthetic-in-alcohol is so *tempting,* in a world that feels like it's collapsing, in a world where you can't see yourself alive in twenty years anyway, but frankly, numbness and dullness lead to death faster and more certain.

Three: Fuck it, you know? I only live once. Every night I spend shitfaced is a night I spend not cooking, or conversing at depth, or making music, and life is nothing without good food, good conversation, and good music.
