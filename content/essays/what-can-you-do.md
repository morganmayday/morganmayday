They're going to kill us all if we don't do something.

We've all seen it. Even if we've tried to ignore it, it's impossible to fully put from your mind. The State Wants To Kill Us. Immigrants in concentration camps, trans women being put on publicly-searchable registries and healthcare being outlawed, the continued panoptic militarisation of the police state powered by AI, record-breaking summers every summer even as data centers are built and built and built. The dying future is here, and the sentiment I hear from almost every voice is "yeah, the world's ending, but I have work tomorrow."

The future isn't dead, yet, though. There is still time---though not an infinite amount. We still have a fighting chance. So: what can you do, here in the dying nation calling itself America?

First, you can show up. The most important thing, bar none, is community and organisation; research your local orgs and work with them. When protests happen, leave your phone at home, cover your tattoos, remove your piercings, wear a mask, and *show up.* When events and fundraisers happen, lend your hands---doesn't matter if you're broke, groups like Food Not Bombs can almost always just use an extra pair of hands to set up and help serve the community.

Second, you can share your skills. I'm [into guns](/grimoire/sharp-edges), I teach every friend who's willing the basics of gun safety. I know the basics of webmastering, I'm teaching that skill to my contacts who can use it. I'm charismatic and cute, I'm talking the girls I meet at the local into meeting others and getting involved. You have skills, too---even if they can't be applied as straightforwardly as mine, almost everything can be used to benefit your community.

Third, you can *learn* skills. I've learned everything I know about guns in the past two years, I [got myself in order](/essays/the-myth-of-self-control) in the past year and a half, I took a Stop the Bleed class for free at my local community center, you can just *learn things.* Every skill you learn is a skill you can share and teach.

Fourth, you can operate more securely. Be more careful about the information you put out, move to encrypted platforms like Signal for day to day communications where you can fight the privacy invasions of apps like Discord and Instagram, encourage others to do the same, switch to less corporate architectures where you can. Privacy is a right, exercise yours! They want to build the Panopticon, *make it a pain in the ass.*

Fifth, you can get armed. This one is less...universally safe, it's expensive, and it requires knowing the law, but someone's got to do it. Armed minorities are inherently harder to genocide. If we aren't capable of shooting back, we're more likely to *have to* shoot back---going after a defenseless group through violence is infinitely easier than going after a well-armed group through violence. Be knowledgeable of the law, don't do anything stupid, connect with likeminded locals, and learn basic safety and first aid. I really, really hope that none of us ever *need* to be armed, but I'd rather have these resources and never need them than need them desperately and not have them.

"I'd rather live kneeling than die standing" no longer applies. Either we die kneeling, under the boot of the Gestapo-Nouveaux in the midst of the climate crash, or we stand up, hand in hand with our sisters, take our chance at life and liberation, and we *give them hell.*
