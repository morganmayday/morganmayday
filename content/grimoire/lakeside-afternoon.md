It's sat on the edge of the little retaining wall, pants rolled up, feet dangling in the water, not making any effort to wash the wet sand from its soles. It stares out towards the other shore, fixated on seemingly nothing.

I settle in beside it, fold an arm around its waist, let its head fall onto my shoulder. I let it think. It needs time to work the words into a usable shape; it'll be done when it's done.

After a minute, it presses its face into my shoulder---I run my hand through its sun-warmed hair---and speaks. 

"God," it starts, "I just want to live."

"You've seen it. We've all seen it. We [wrote about it](/essays/what-can-you-do), about not feeling helpless in the face of it. And yet..." Adelaide flops its head back up, kicks at the water. "Fuck, Leylune. I want this to be someone else's fight. I want to _relax._"

"We've been relaxing," I point out. "We can keep pacing ourselves."

"Can we?" She sits all the way up, runs her hand through her hair. "Shit's going to be _bad_ soon. The news, the---the _genocide,_ and nobody's coming to save us. We have to do it ourselves.I feel this---this *drag,* this *burn,* this need to be a beacon, exemplar, what-fucking-have-you, to do it *all*, because at least this way it'll get *done.*"

"It won't get done if you get yourself killed." I tug her back into my grasp. She doesn't protest. "And besides, you working yourself to death wears on us, too. So don't."

My sister exhales, after another moment. "...I'm scared."

"Yeah."

"It's all so fucking terrifying."

"Yeah."

"And there's nothing I can do but bide my time, contribute where I can, and try very hard to manage my energy."

"Yeah."

"...fuck." It shifts in my arms. "No comfort? No sweet words for me? No *everything will be OK?*"

I don't answer. I let the words I want to say unknot themselves, and watch the water.

"...Adelaide," I begin, "we both know that we might likely be dead this time next year. We're doing what we can to change those odds for the better, but some things are beyond our grasp. We'll make it out as far as we can, with ourselves and our loved ones. That's the only thing I can promise you. I can't promise we'll be safe, I can't promise things will be OK, I can't promise a better future at all. But even if we die, even if things go to hell and the world fucking ends, I'll be hand in hand with you."
